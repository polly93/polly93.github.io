ENVIRONMENTAL POLICY · STRUCTURAL IO

Plastic Bottle Policy Simulation

A structural demand and counterfactual analysis of bans and taxes on small plastic bottles.

PROBLEM

How do environmental policies reshape consumer and firm outcomes?

The analysis evaluates how restrictions on small plastic bottles affect product substitution, prices, consumer welfare, firm profits, and material use.

DATA & WORKFLOW

Building an analysis-ready dataset

  • Constructed product-market data for differentiated beverages.
  • Combined sales, product characteristics, prices, and cost shifters.
  • Prepared policy scenarios involving taxes, bans, package-size changes, and material substitution.

METHOD

Random-coefficients demand and counterfactual simulation

Estimated differentiated-product demand using GMM and simulated market equilibria under alternative policy and firm-response scenarios.

INTERPRETATION

What the analysis contributes

The project highlights the ability to connect economic modeling to concrete policy trade-offs rather than reporting a single outcome metric.
