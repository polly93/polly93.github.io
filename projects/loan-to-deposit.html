BANKING REGULATION · MARKET ANALYSIS

Loan-to-Deposit Regulation and Internet-Only Bank Lending

Cross-checked loan-to-deposit ratios across multiple sources, visualized the current landscape, and explored how easing corporate-lending constraints could affect the business models and loan portfolios of internet-only banks.

View analysis View repository

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Connecting a prudential ratio to competitive strategy

The project began with a practical question: how do loan-to-deposit requirements shape banks’ capacity to expand lending, and could regulatory flexibility allow internet-only banks to diversify beyond their existing retail-focused portfolios?

Question

How do reported loan-to-deposit ratios differ across institutions and sources?

Policy angle

What directional changes might follow from easing corporate-lending constraints?

Output

A source-validated comparison, descriptive visualization, and exploratory scenario review.

Interpretation

Descriptive and exploratory—not a causal estimate or a balance-sheet forecast.

DATA & VALIDATION

Cross-checking the ratio across multiple sources

Loan-to-deposit figures can vary because sources may use different reporting dates, institution classifications, accounting scopes, or regulatory definitions. I therefore compared figures from several sources rather than relying on a single reported number.

1. Collect

Gathered institution-level and sector-level figures from multiple public sources.

2. Align

Checked reporting periods, units, coverage, and the definition used for the ratio.

3. Reconcile

Documented discrepancies and retained comparable observations for visualization.

4. Visualize

Converted the validated comparison into a chart for bank- and group-level review.

DESCRIPTIVE VIEW

Visualizing the current loan-to-deposit landscape

The chart is intended to make cross-bank differences visible and to identify where internet-only banks sit relative to other institutions. It should be read together with the source and reporting-date notes.

EXPLORATORY POLICY SCENARIO

What could change if corporate-lending constraints were eased?

I conducted a directional review of how greater regulatory room for corporate lending could affect internet-only banks. The exercise focused on possible mechanisms rather than predicting a specific change in lending volume.

Portfolio diversification

Greater access to corporate and small-business lending could reduce concentration in household credit.

New customer segments

Digital underwriting and transaction data could support expansion into SMEs and sole proprietors.

Funding and liquidity

Loan growth would still need to remain consistent with deposit funding, liquidity management, and prudential requirements.

Risk-management demands

Corporate lending would require stronger credit models, monitoring processes, and sector-level risk controls.

INTERPRETATION

A regulatory change can create capacity, but not guarantee expansion

The analysis suggests that easing corporate-lending constraints could create room for internet-only banks to broaden their portfolios and competitive positioning. Actual outcomes, however, would depend on funding capacity, capital and liquidity, borrower demand, underwriting capability, risk appetite, and market competition.

Analytical limitation: This was a descriptive source-comparison and exploratory scenario analysis. It does not estimate a causal regulatory effect or forecast a bank’s balance sheet under a formal structural model.
