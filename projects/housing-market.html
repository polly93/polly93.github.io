HOUSING MARKET ANALYTICS

Do Local Real Estate Brokers Affect Housing Prices?

A transaction-level study of broker location, local information, and apartment resale prices in Seoul.

PROBLEM

Can broker geography reveal information frictions?

The project asks whether the locations of buyer- and seller-side brokers are systematically associated with transaction prices, after accounting for property characteristics and fixed effects.

DATA & WORKFLOW

Building an analysis-ready dataset

  • Integrated transaction, broker, regional, and geographic data.
  • Created local/nonlocal broker classifications and market-comparison features.
  • Prepared a reduced-form estimation sample of 170,744 broker-mediated transactions.

METHOD

Hedonic panel regression

Estimated log-price specifications with property controls, time fixed effects, apartment fixed effects, and broker-location indicators. Results are treated as observational associations rather than causal estimates.

INTERPRETATION

What the analysis contributes

The project demonstrates end-to-end data engineering, feature construction, fixed-effects modeling, empirical judgment, and the ability to communicate both findings and identification limits.
